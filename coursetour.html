Course Tour

Hole 1

Hole 1 is a 391 yard par 4 that has a slight dogleg left. A good drive in the fairway sets up an early birdie opportunity. But beware of the overhanging trees on the right if its a back-left pin.

Hole 2

Hole 2 is a 521 yard par 5 with a slight dogleg to the right. The fairway is wide, but beware of the fairway bunker on the right. Longer hitters will have no problem getting home in 2, but water does surround the left,back, and right sides of the green

Hole 3

Hole 3 is the start of a tough 2 hole stretch. Measuring at 217 yards this par 3 is all you want.Any tee shot that finds the green on this hole is no small task.

Hole 4

Hole 4 is a long, difficult par 4 measuring at 440 yards. A fairway bunker on the right side of the fairway and ob left add more trouble to this hole. Pars on 3 and 4 aren't going to hurt you.

Hole 5

Hole 5 is a 184 yard par-3 with bunkers surrounding a smaller green. A tee shot to the middle of this green is a quality golf shot.

Hole 6

Hole 6 seems like a different hole from the blue tees. Other teeboxes have a straight shot at the fairway, but a draw is required to find the fairway from the blues. If you find the fairway this 376 par 4 is another birdie opportunity.

Hole 7

Hole 7 is a straightaway par 4 measuring at 429 yards. Beware of the collection area short right, as any approach shot finding the left side of the green can easily end up in this dangerous area.

Hole 8

Much like hole 8 this 534 yard par 5 requires a draw from the blue tees. A 4-tiered green awaits your approach shot, so pay close attention to the flag placement.

Hole 9

Hole 9 is a great risk/reward hole to close out the front side. A 346 yard par 4 that allows you to hit the big stick or layup short of the fairway bunkers on the right side. This green has a severe slope from back to front, you do not want to be putting from above the hole.

Hole 10

The backside starts with a great birdie opportunity if you find the fairway. Hole 10 measures in at 349 yards and has a slight dogleg to the right. Any tee ball that doesn't find the fairway is sure to have tree trouble.

Hole 11

Hole 11 is a beautiful 170 yard par 3. A small pond and bunkers line the left side and the green snakes around these areas. A back-left pin requires you to carry your ball over both of the hazards.

Hole 12

Hole 12 is a straightaway, 385 yard par 4. Trees creep in on both sides of the fairway, and the approach shot will be hit into one of the trickiest greens on the course.

Hole 13

As you cross the train tracks another birdie opportunity awaits. Hole 13 is a 518 yard par 5 with a fairway bunker on the right. Long hitters can fly the bunker while others will need to play to the left of it.

Hole 14

Hole 14 is a 165 yard par 4 with water all down the right side. If the pin is on the right side a shot to the center of the green is the smart, safe choice.

Hole 15

Hole 15 is another long, straightaway par 4 measuring at 412 yards. Miss the fairway bunker on the right and an approach to the center of the green will set you up for a par.

Hole 16

Hole 16 is a straightaway par 4 measuring at 373 yards. A wide landing area and relatively flat green makes this a birdie opportunity. Water does wrap around the left, back, and right sides of the green.

Hole 17

Hole 17 is a 528 yard par 5 that doglegs slightly to the right. A deep fairway bunker on the left hand side must be avoided. Another great birdie opportunity.

Hole 18

Hole 18 is one of the toughest closing holes in Kansas. Measuring at 382 yards, length isn't the issue here. A creek guards the fairway 230 yards off of the tee. Long hitters can cut the corner, but the landing area over the creek is small. Par is a great score here.
